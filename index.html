summary

This review was published in 2018 in Trends in Cancer. This corresponding author is Prof.Mauricio Berriel Diaz.In this review authors reinforeced the idea that cachexia syndrome is not only about skeletal muscle wasting, but a multi-tissue syndrome.

glossary

  • Acute-phase response (APR): a prominent systemic reaction of the organism to local or systemic disturbances in its homeostasis caused by infection, tissue injury, trauma, surgery, neoplastic growth, or immunological disorders. It represents an unspecific and acute inflammatory response comprising the synthesis of acute-phase proteins primarily by hepatocytes, which are released into the circulation.
  • Brown adipose tissue(BAT): possesses a number of specialized features enabling it to function as a thermoregulatory organ by generating heat. Brown adipocytes display a high density of mitochondria that are characterized by the unique presence of UCP1.
  • Uncoupling protein 1 (UCP1): located in the inner mitochondrial membrane where it mediates the translocation of protons into the mitochondrial matrix, thereby bypassing proton re-entry via the ATP synthase. Consequently, the energy of the proton gradient is dissipated as heat. The UCP1mediated proton leak lowers the membrane potential, which increases electron flux through the respiratory chain, establishing a futile cycle able to produce considerable amounts of heat, thus increasing energy expenditure

The multi-tissue syndrome of cancer cachexia:

Adipose tissue

The authors talk about the function about adipose tissue in three ways:

The first idea(also more well-accepted)is that adipose tissue loss often hapen before muscle loss.In cancer cachexia, the adipose tissue will undergo strong remodeling, like lipid depletion, extracellular matrix remodeling and enhanced lipolysis activities. cachexia patients oftern observe increased FFA(free fatty acids) in plasma. Enzymes involved in lipolysis like HSL, ATGL also have higher activities.

In the second part, they present the controversy that whether weight loss in cachexia is directly related to increased energy expenditure, and more specifically, the activities of the BAT.In our body, BAT(brown adpise tissue) is responsible for thermogenesis while WAT is for energy storage.Therefore, it may be natural to think that cancer cachexia patient will have more BAT because of their dramatic “energy” loss. That’s the controversial one actually. On the one hand, brown of beige(brown in white) AT marker like CIDE-A and UCP-1 are indeed observed in cachexia. However, researchers couldn’t get consistent results among different mouse models. And most strikingly, UCP-1 knockout and Î²-adrenergic blockcage mice still develop cachexia. Therefore, the exact answer remains to be explored. The authors propose that alternative futile energy cycles(for example, the creatine kinase cycle) whichi is UCP-1 independent may play a more importat role in this process.

The third part they talks about the systemic effects of AT dysfunction. For instances, poor lipid storage may lead to lipotoxicity and change the levels of adipolines, and lipokines.

Heart

cachexia is also strongly related to heart muscle wasting.Note that some heart illness such as chronic hearta failure can also cause cachexia(specifically referred to as cardiac cachexia).The heart funciton is impaired: reduced fractional shortening(reduced heart rate, impaired cardiac contractility).

cardiac cacheixa also show fibrosis and readopts a fetal gene expression program. But whether the autophagy or proteasome degradation play a more important role remains future validation(C-26 model).

liver

liver is an important metabolic organ. . Researches have shown the lactate produced by the tumor cells can participate int the lactate cycle and this cause a huge energy loss in cancer condition.

Besides, cachexia mice show reduced ketogenic potential and decreased levels of PPARÎ± in the liver., which leads to increased glucocorticoid levels. PPARÎ± agonist could prevent muscle wasting in tumor-bearing mice.

decreased TG mobilization and hepatic steatosis was also observed in differnet murine cancer cachexia models. And this would contribute to insulin resistance but the data is quite limited.

Also, the cachexia is related to hepatic APR activation for pancreatic or colon cancer. But the direct evidence is lacking.

Other tisses

  • Brain: decreased food intake, altered neuronal output?
  • gut: gut microiota change, gut barrier dysfuntion.
  • pancreas: diabete mellitus(pancreatic cancer cells may secrete exosomes containing adrenomedulie to intefere insulin signaling). (endocrine/exocrine function may be changed).
  • bone: bone loss, increased TGF-Î² may play a role in that in bone metastasis model.

Concluding

There are two layers of variables of regulation in cancer cachexia: the various tissue and various degress of metabolic dysregulation. The cancer studies should transit to a more systemic level other than just focusing on the local environment.
